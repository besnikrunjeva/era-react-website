## Brand DNA Summary

ERA is Kosovo's food-service packaging specialist — 15 years, 397 partners, 3.9M units/year — speaking warm-professional B2B to café and restaurant owners as peers (informal "ti", no hype, short sentences). The visual identity is dark and premium: ERA green `#4ca706` on near-black `#0f1010`, signalling "regional manufacturing partner," not "local print shop." The single biggest differentiator is the only live 3D configurator in the region: a café owner can drop their logo onto a cup in the browser and see it before committing to a 500-pc minimum order.

**Voice axes (interpreted from profile):** warm 7/10 · confident/bold 7/10 · formal 4/10 (informal "ti", peer-to-peer) · hype 1/10.

---

## Messaging Pillars

These four angles map across every platform and every concept. Each ad should lead with one pillar, not all four.

1. **The 3D Configurator (hero differentiator).** "See your logo before you order." The only browser-based 3D product previewer in Kosovo. Removes the #1 objection — "I can't picture how my brand will look." This is the traffic magnet and the reason to click.
2. **Local Manufacturer (trust + speed).** Made in Kosovo. Fast delivery, direct relationship, no middlemen, no long international lead times. Talk to the people who actually print it.
3. **Brand Identity (the aspiration).** Generic white cups make a café look like everyone else. Custom-printed packaging turns every takeaway coffee into a walking advertisement. This is about how the owner's business *looks* to their own customers.
4. **Social Proof (credibility at scale).** 397 partners · 15 years · 3.9M units/year. Accessible 500-pc minimum so even a small café can start. Big enough to trust, small enough to care.

## Campaign Concepts

### Concept 1: The Logo Reveal
**Hypothesis:** The configurator's "see it before you order" moment is inherently visual and novel in this market — showing the logo *appearing* on a cup is the highest-curiosity, most thumb-stopping idea ERA owns.
**Primary Message:** Drop your logo onto a cup and see it live, in your browser, before you order a single piece.
**Tone:** warm 7/10, confident 7/10, informal "ti".
**Copy Framework:** BAB (Before → blank generic cup; After → branded cup; Bridge → the configurator).
**Visual Direction A (Photography):** banana Product mode. A single takeaway coffee cup on dark `#0f1010` surface, shot in soft directional light, brand logo crisp on the side, a faint `#4ca706` rim glow. Premium product-hero feel, condensation detail, zero clutter.
**Visual Direction B (Illustration):** banana UI/Web mode. Abstract dark-mode browser frame, a cup silhouette rotating mid-frame, a logo shape snapping into place with a soft green pulse. Implies "configurator" without literal UI text.
**Target Platforms:** TikTok + Instagram Reels (the reveal is motion-native), Meta Feed for the static cut.
**CTA:** Shiko logon tënde — era-printpack.com
**Addresses:** General brand awareness + the #1 differentiator (no audit weakness to target).

### Concept 2: Made Here, Made Fast
**Hypothesis:** Café owners are burned by long lead times and distant suppliers; "local manufacturer, talk to us directly" is a trust lever competitors who resell can't match.
**Primary Message:** Made in Kosovo by the people who actually print it — fast delivery, direct line, no middlemen.
**Tone:** warm 7/10, confident 8/10, grounded and plain-spoken.
**Copy Framework:** PAS (Problem: slow foreign suppliers / generic stock → Agitate → Solution: local maker, fast turnaround).
**Visual Direction A (Photography):** banana Portrait mode. A real-feeling production-floor or owner portrait — hands holding a freshly printed branded cup, machinery softly blurred behind, warm light against the dark palette. Human, proud, regional-partner energy.
**Visual Direction B (Illustration):** banana Abstract mode. Dark map-of-Kosovo silhouette with a `#4ca706` route line pulsing from a single point outward to many — fast, local distribution as an abstract metaphor, no labels.
**Target Platforms:** Facebook (older café/restaurant owner skew), Instagram Feed, Google Search (for "kuti/gota me printim Kosovë" intent).
**CTA:** Merr ofertë falas në WhatsApp
**Addresses:** Audience pain point — long lead times + difficulty finding a local custom-print supplier.

### Concept 3: 500 Is Enough
**Hypothesis:** The accessible 500-pc minimum directly dissolves the "I'm too small to order custom" objection — naming the number is more persuasive than any adjective.
**Primary Message:** You don't need to be a chain. Start with 500 pieces and put your brand on every cup.
**Tone:** warm 8/10, encouraging, peer-to-peer.
**Copy Framework:** PAS (Problem: "custom is only for big chains" → Solution: 500-pc minimum).
**Visual Direction A (Photography):** banana Product mode. A small, tidy stack of branded cups beside a single espresso on a café counter, `#0f1010` tones, `#4ca706` accent on the cup print. Feels attainable, not corporate.
**Visual Direction B (Illustration):** banana Abstract mode. A small cluster of cup shapes vs. an implied larger field, the small cluster glowing green — "small order, big look" visual metaphor, no numerals rendered in-image.
**Target Platforms:** Instagram + Facebook Feed (objection-handling reads better static), Meta retargeting for site visitors who didn't convert.
**CTA:** Merr ofertë falas në WhatsApp
**Addresses:** Audience pain point — accessible minimum quantity objection (strong retargeting concept).

### Concept 4: Every Cup Is a Billboard
**Hypothesis:** Reframing packaging as marketing (not a cost) speaks to the owner's growth motivation — a branded cup walks out the door and advertises the café all day.
**Primary Message:** Your customer carries your brand down the street. Make the cup work for you.
**Tone:** confident 8/10, warm 6/10, a touch aspirational but never hyped.
**Copy Framework:** FAB (Feature: full-CMYK custom print → Advantage: your logo everywhere → Benefit: free walking advertising).
**Visual Direction A (Photography):** banana Portrait mode. Lifestyle — a customer walking a city street holding a branded ERA-printed cup, logo facing camera, shallow depth of field, dark cinematic grade with a `#4ca706` accent in frame.
**Visual Direction B (Illustration):** banana Abstract mode. Multiple cup silhouettes scattered across a dark cityscape outline, each emitting a small green glow — brand reach as ambient light, no text.
**Target Platforms:** TikTok + Reels (lifestyle motion), Instagram Feed, Google PMax for broad reach.
**CTA:** Shiko logon tënde — era-printpack.com
**Addresses:** General brand awareness + brand-identity motivation.

### Concept 5: 15 Years, 397 Partners
**Hypothesis:** In a trust-sensitive B2B purchase against a 50-year competitor, concrete proof numbers earn the click better than claims — and they reassure at the retarget/convert stage.
**Primary Message:** 397 cafés and restaurants already print with us. Join them.
**Tone:** confident 8/10, warm 6/10, credible and calm.
**Copy Framework:** 4P (Promise → Picture → Proof: the numbers → Push).
**Visual Direction A (Photography):** banana Product mode. A confident flat-lay or grid of varied branded cups and boxes (different fictional café styles) on dark backdrop — range + volume implied visually, `#4ca706` thread tying them together.
**Visual Direction B (Illustration):** banana Abstract mode. Dark field of many small dots/cup-marks, a dense `#4ca706` constellation — "397 partners" as a visual density metaphor, numerals added later as overlay copy, not baked into the image.
**Target Platforms:** Facebook + Instagram Feed, Meta retargeting (closing), Google Search brand-defense.
**CTA:** Shiko produktet — era-printpack.com
**Addresses:** Social proof / competitive differentiation vs. Shtypshkronja SHPK (strong retargeting + closing concept).

## Platform Strategy & Creative Direction

### Meta (Facebook + Instagram)
**Role:** Core traffic + retargeting engine. Carries all five concepts.
**Audience targeting:**
- Cold: interests — café owners, restaurant management, food & beverage industry, small-business owners; behaviours — small business owners / page admins; geo — Kosovo (all), Albania (Tirana, Durrës, Prishtina-adjacent metros). Age 25–55.
- Lookalike: 1–3% LAL from website visitors and WhatsApp clickers once pixel data accrues.
- Retarget: site visitors 30/90-day, configurator/`/products/gota` viewers, video viewers 50%+, Instagram/FB page engagers 365-day.
**Creative feel:** Dark, premium, product-forward. Static product heroes (Concept 1, 3, 5) for Feed; Reels for the logo reveal and lifestyle cuts (Concept 1, 4). Albanian primary, English variant for diaspora business audiences. Keep the `#4ca706` accent consistent so the brand is recognisable across the funnel.
**Placements:** Feed (4:5), Reels/Stories (9:16), keep copy zones clear per the safe-zone notes below.

### TikTok
**Role:** Top-of-funnel awareness + virality engine. The configurator reveal is native to the platform.
**Audience targeting:** Kosovo + Albania, 22–45. Interests: small business, food & drink, entrepreneurship, café/barista content. Lean on broad targeting + strong hooks (TikTok rewards creative over tight targeting). Layer a custom audience exclusion of existing site visitors for prospecting sets.
**Creative feel:** Native, hand-held, founder/owner POV — NOT polished ad spots. Screen-recordings of the configurator in action perform best (the "wait, you can do this in the browser?" moment). Albanian voiceover or on-screen captions, fast first 2 seconds, ERA green only as an accent. Show, don't sell.
**Placements:** In-feed 9:16, Spark Ads from an ERA TikTok handle for authenticity.

### Google Ads
**Role:** Capture existing intent (Search) + cheap reach/retargeting (PMax/Display).
**Audience & structure:**
- **Search:** brand-defense campaign (bid on "ERA print pack", "Shtypshkronja ERA") + non-brand intent ("gota letre me printim", "kuti hamburgeri personalizuara", "ambalazhe ushqimi Kosovë", "paper cups custom Kosovo"). Note from SEO audit: query-first AL phrasing matters — mirror "ambalazhe letre / gota me printim Kosovë" in headlines.
- **PMax:** one asset group per product cluster (cups, boxes, accessories), feed the dark product imagery + green-accent assets; let it find configurator-curious users cheaply.
- **Display retargeting:** site + configurator visitors with Concept 3 (500-pc reassurance) and Concept 5 (social proof).
**Creative feel:** Responsive Search Ads with the Albanian headlines below; Display banners reuse Concept 1/5 product heroes with right-third kept open for Google overlay.

## TikTok Video Concept Hooks

First 2 seconds decide everything. Each hook is built to stop the scroll for a café owner.

1. **The browser reveal (highest priority).** Open mid-screen-recording: a blank white cup, then the owner's logo dragged on and the cup rotating in 3D. Hook line: *"Po e vendos logon time mbi gotë… në browser."* Payoff: "Vetëm tek ERA."
2. **The blank-cup callout.** Hook on a generic unbranded café cup: *"Kjo gotë po të kushton klientë."* Cut to a branded ERA cup. Reframes plain packaging as a missed marketing opportunity.
3. **POV owner unboxing.** First-person: a café owner opens a delivery of their freshly printed cups for the first time. Hook: *"Hapa kutinë me gotat e mia të reja…"* Authentic reaction, founder energy.
4. **The "I thought I was too small" myth.** Hook: *"Mendoja që printimi është vetëm për zinxhirët e mëdhenj."* Reveal the 500-pc minimum. Objection-killer for micro-cafés.
5. **Speed flex.** Hook: *"Sa shpejt? Prodhues vendor — pa pritje nga jashtë."* Quick montage of local production → delivery. Sells the local-manufacturer pillar in motion.
6. **Before/after counter.** Split: left a plain takeaway counter, right the same counter with branded cups, holders, and boxes. Hook: *"E njëjta kafe. Dy biznese të ndryshme."*

All TikTok cuts end on the same card: configurator URL + WhatsApp, ERA green on dark.
